## Current work

**Deterministic Gap Bounds for Layered Attention**  
*June 2026–present*

This project studies the difference between a deployed transformer block, viewed as a finite ordered composition of residual sublayer maps, and the effective continuous or averaged dynamics often used in mathematical analyses of attention. The starting point is the interacting-particle viewpoint on self-attention dynamics, especially work on clustering and mean-field limits. My focus is the finite-depth deterministic error: how much is lost when replacing the actual ordered composition by an effective flow, and how the leading ordering error is controlled by Lie brackets of the attention and feed-forward vector fields.

## Completed projects

**Commutator-Driven Stability Bounds for Periodic Switching**  
*September 2025–May 2026*

This project gives explicit stability guarantees for periodically switched linear systems by comparing the true switched evolution with an averaged system. The main estimates use commutator bounds to quantify the discrepancy caused by noncommuting subsystem generators, producing certified switching-period conditions for inheriting exponential stability from the averaged dynamics.

Links: [IEEE](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11589288), [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05829), [code](https://github.com/dmallik17/Commutator_Driven_Stability_Bounds)

**On the Midpoint Scheme for Lie–Poisson Dynamics**  
*May 2025–August 2025*

This project studies the implicit midpoint method for Lie–Poisson systems, focusing on its structure-preserving behavior. The report works through the Poisson bracket formalism, explains why midpoint integration preserves linear and quadratic invariants in this setting, and illustrates the method through rigid-body and unicycle examples.

Link: [PDF](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1WZFXBe4DHdlKl4FBM4kJbUpd8yd8Dapj/view)

**On the Differential of the Exponential Map**  
*May 2025–August 2025*

This project derives formulas for the differential of the matrix exponential. Starting from Taylor expansion, it obtains integral representations, adjoint-action formulas, and the Fréchet derivative viewpoint. The report is a useful bridge between matrix analysis, Lie brackets, and the noncommutative expansions that later appear in switching and splitting problems.

Link: [PDF](https://drive.google.com/file/d/148JRVoAmbJ43hKh8RUdUbjqGizp8KB4F/view)

**Spectrahedral Attitude Estimation and Convex MPC**  
*November 2025-December 2025*

This course project combines convex optimization and geometric control for rigid-body attitude problems. It formulates Wahba’s problem using a spectrahedral lift of $SO(3)$, recovers attitude through a quaternion/eigenvector construction, and then regulates the estimated target using a bounded-torque convex MPC formulation in intrinsic error coordinates.

Link: [PDF](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1z9FpBMvRmpwMwcC1DGKA31IpjPBfraEF/view)
